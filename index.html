Projects

I like working on random projects. Having been a loyal and obsessive reader most of my life, it's been very exhilarating to working on creative projects. Whether it's extracting meaning and insights out of large amounts of data or visualizing them to a broader audience, I learnt a lot working on them.

Here's a sample of them...

Visualizing UCLA Stats Program (2016)

I made this interactive visualization of the UCLA Stats program to help students select classes and plan for the major or minor. Working on this project improved my design intuition and ability to visually communicate complex information.

This was the first web app that I built "from scratch", using vanilla JavaScript, D3.js, Bootstrap CSS and straightforward HTML.

I shared it with more than 700 Stats Club members and other UCLA students and got really positive feedback. This also got me my latest gig at the student newspaper as a front end developer. I will continually update the data as well as add features.

Kaggle SF Crime Contest (2015)

I participated in this machine learning contest to work with socio-geographic data and build predictive models. The biggest challenge was to estimate a probability distribution for each unseen observation, rather than just a one-shot classification.

In English: I had to quantify every possibility as remote as it might be by treating outliers with respect.

As computing speed and my own time became precious, I needed to deal with these constraints through sampling and feature engineering.
