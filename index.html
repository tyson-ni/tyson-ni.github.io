About me

How could I help a new Uber team get off to a great start? When I interned at Uber, I built a dashboard for a newly established team so that they could better understand past research and champion the value of their work throughout the company. My dashboard featured a rigorous presentation of data insights and intuitive interface and graphics, bringing visibility into fragmented data.

How are academic subjects related? I scraped course descriptions and applied natural language processing algorithms to dissect the semantic relationships between various subjects. Later I would turn that analysis into an interactive article to help students understand how their areas of interest relate to others'.

How long did my puppy bark after we left him home alone? I placed a recorder at home, and after getting back I wrote a Python script to analyze the 3-hour-long audio. As it turned out, he was naughty for two minutes, but quieted down soon after, to my relief.

Projects

Here are some of my favorites:

UCLA Gym Traffic (2016)

Using entrance data of UCLA facilities, I helped students pick the right time and place to go work out. This was my proudest project and it involved: writing a proposal to request data, simulating traffic patterns using statistics, and making responsive graphics on the frontend.

Visualizing Stats Program (2016)

I helped statistics students pick classes and plan for their major.

Easy Classes (2016)

I analyzed the grade distribution data and made interactive tools to help students pick GE classes.

Data Visualization Workshop (2016)

I ran a workshop for Stats Club members, introducing them to data visualization.

Kaggle Crime Contest (2015)

The biggest challenge in this machine learning project was to infer a distribution for each observation, rather than merely provide a single classification. Having done other Kaggle competitions, I learn the most from this.

Air Sensor Dashboard (2015)

I built a dashboard to visualize CO2 level, temperature and humidity. It also allowed users to examine the relationship between any two variables and interactively lookup the raw data.

UCLA DataFest (2015)

Three teammates and I participated in UCLA's annual data hackathon where we analyzed car sales data. This weekend kickstarted my passion for writing code to solve data problems.

IBM Watson Case Presentation (2014)

I worked with two teammates to envison deploying the IBM Watson AI in Chase bank branches.

Honduras Microfinance Trip (2014)

I was part of a group of ten students who traveled to a Honduran village to learn from and advise a local microfinance co-op.

Bathrooms of UCLA (2015)

Inspired by a blog post on a fun type of graphics called Chernoff Faces, I rated bathrooms at UCLA and used facial features to represent variables like cleanliness, privacy, etc.
